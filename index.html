Palletizing

When your business decides to automate its palletizing process, there are essentially two choices: robotic or conventional.  IPA can work with you to examine and evaluate your company's needs.

A robotic palletizer offers several key advantages:

  1. Typically takes up less space
  2. Can adapt easier to accommodating different pallet patterns and product types
  3. Can handle up to 1000 kg (2,200 lbs)
  4. Can handle many different product types, including cases, bags, pails, bottles, batteries, cubes of scrap metal, and plastic containers
  5. Can simultaneously handle multiple infeeds of different SKUs
  6. A single articulated arm can typically handle up to 4 different SKU’s.  If the articulated arm attached to a moving track or a gantry style robot is utilized, 8 to 12 SKU’s can be handled simultaneously by a single robot. This of course depends on important criteria, such as pallet stacking patterns and case rates.
  7. Price can sometimes be less, especially when looking at four or more simultaneous infeeds.
  8. Palletizing rates can vary anywhere from 8 to 30 cases/min for a single robot depending on stacking patterns, requirements, and if the robot is picking a single product, rows of product, or full layer of products.
  9. Mixed case palletizing is possible

A conventional palletizer is made up of the following key components:

  • Infeed conveyor
  • Case turner (if case rotation is required)
  • Row forming conveyor with integrated pusher
  • Layer forming table with stripper plate and layer-squaring device
  • Elevator that lifts full layers of products
  • Pallet dispensers
  • Slip/Tier sheet dispensers
  • Full load pallet discharge conveyor

Training

IPA supplies robot technicians certified by the robot manufacturer for the training of end-user personnel. Our training is offered in two formats, Informal and Formal Training.


Informal Training

Informal Training is performed on every cell and includes practical training conducted immediately as the last stage of the start-up process. Informal training includes:

  • Electrical, mechanical, and pneumatic systems
  • System Operation including Programming, Changing Products, End-of Run, Recovery, Screens, Navigation, and Application Parameters
  • System Troubleshooting
  • Preventative Maintenance
  • Safety Training

A printed manual is included beyond the manuals included on-board the robot controller. This manual contains best practices and quick-guides to expedite the learning process, as well as a complete recommended Spare Parts List.


Formal Training

Formal Training is performed optionally and includes both classroom and hands-on training at our production facility. This program is customized to the client’s needs and can incorporate formal verification techniques.
